Experiment 4: Electromagnet Strength Test

Have you ever wondered how magnets can be made with electricity? In this experiment, you’ll create an electromagnet using a nail, wire, and battery. By changing the number of wire coils around the nail, you’ll test how it affects the magnet’s strength.

What You’ll Do

Build a simple electromagnet and test how its strength changes based on the number of wire coils wrapped around the nail. This will give you insight into the relationship between electricity and magnetism.

What You’ll Need


  • Iron nail (3–4 inches long)

  • Insulated copper wire (3–4 feet long)

  • Battery (D-cell or 9V)

  • Paper clips or small metal objects

  • Electrical tape

Steps to Follow

  1. Wrap the Wire Around the Nail:
    • Coil the wire tightly around the nail, leaving 3 inches free on each end.
  2. Connect to the Battery:
    • Strip insulation from the wire ends and attach them to the battery terminals.
  3. Test Your Electromagnet:
    • Use the magnet to pick up paper clips or metal objects.
  4. Experiment with More Coils:
    • Add more coils and observe changes in magnetic strength.

What’s Happening?

Electricity flowing through the wire creates a magnetic field, turning the nail into an electromagnet. More coils increase the magnetic field's concentration and strength.

Try These Fun Variations

  • Test different battery voltages.
  • Use nails of different sizes.
  • Measure magnet strength with a spring scale.

Tips for Success

  • Use fresh batteries for better results.
  • Ensure coils are tightly wrapped.
  • Disconnect the battery when not in use.

Why It’s Cool: Learn how electricity creates magnetic fields, a principle used in many modern technologies!
